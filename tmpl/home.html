What is Kigo?

Best case scenario it is a place for reading and sharing haiku. But, being no stranger to fringe weblogs, my guess is it'll quickly devolve into a place for troglodytic internet-users to express themselves psychosexually. Either way--enjoy!

A kigo (季語 "season word") is a word or phrase meant to evoke the season in which a poem is set. E.g., cherry blossoms are a common spring kigo in japanese poetry, whereas the moon is strongly associated with autumn. I partly chose the name because it has the substr "go" in it, which is the language in which this website was written. But also I thought it sounded cool. And isn't that reason enough?

What are Haiku?

A haiku is a form of short unrhymed Japanese poem typically constrained to three lines containing 5, 7, and 5 syllables, respectively. A haiku, by nature, is a distillation of an experience: it is a mood and a moment in time. It reflects a fresh way of seeing that is unselfconscious/childlike. It deals strictly in the immediate: any authorial interpretation ought to be omitted. Most importantly, its language should be resonant, natural, and succinct.
